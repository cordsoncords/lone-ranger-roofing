The storm passes through Belton, the sky clears, and most homeowners walk outside to check on the yard, the car, and the fence before anyone thinks to look up. That's understandable. Roof damage doesn't announce itself the way a broken window does, and a lot of it stays invisible from the ground until a leak shows up weeks later.

We've inspected enough Belton roofs after enough storms to put together a practical rundown of what to check first, and where the limits of a homeowner's own look-around actually are. None of this replaces a professional inspection, but it gives you a clear read on whether one needs to happen soon or right away.

## Start With What You Can See From the Ground

Walk the perimeter of the house and look at the roofline from a few different angles. Missing or visibly displaced shingles are the easiest sign to catch this way, along with any granules collecting in the gutters or at the base of downspouts, which usually means shingles are shedding their protective coating faster than normal. Check the gutters and downspouts themselves for dents, since hail that's hard enough to dent metal gutters is usually hard enough to bruise shingles too.

Look at any metal flashing you can see around chimneys, vents, and skylights. Dents or visible gaps here are worth photographing, because flashing failures are a common source of leaks that don't show up until the next rain.

## Check the Yard for Corroborating Evidence

The yard tells part of the story too. Dented outdoor furniture, damaged AC units, or dings on wood fencing all point to hail impact strong enough to affect the roof as well. If you find granules scattered across the driveway or patio after a storm, that's a strong sign your roof lost some of its protective surface.

This evidence matters beyond confirming there was a storm. It becomes part of the documentation an insurance adjuster looks for when evaluating a claim, since damage to multiple surfaces around the property supports the idea that the roof took a comparable hit.

## Check Inside the House

Head up to the attic if you have access, and look for water stains, damp insulation, or daylight coming through anywhere it shouldn't. These are signs that water has already found a way in, even if nothing looks wrong on the roof itself. Check ceilings in upper-floor rooms for new discoloration or soft spots, since a leak can travel some distance from where it entered before it shows up as a stain.

## What You Can't Safely Check Yourself

Climbing onto the roof after a storm is where we'd stop a homeowner's checklist. Wet or damaged shingles are unstable footing, and the parts of the roof most likely to show storm damage, like bruised mat material under the granules or cracked seals around vents, aren't visible without getting close and, in some cases, testing the surface directly. A [roof inspection](/residential-roofing/roof-inspection) covers this safely and documents it the way an insurance claim actually needs.

## Why Timing Matters After a Storm

A roof with storm damage doesn't always leak right away. Cracked mat material and compromised flashing seals can sit for months before a hard rain finds the weak point, and by then the damage has often spread to decking and insulation, turning a contained repair into a bigger job. Belton homeowners who get an inspection scheduled soon after a storm, even without an obvious leak, tend to catch problems while they're still small and clearly tied to a specific event for insurance purposes.

## FAQ

**How soon after a storm should I get my roof inspected in Belton?**
Within a week or two if possible, especially after hail or high wind. Damage that isn't leaking yet can still be documented clearly while it's fresh and clearly tied to the storm.

**Can I do a full roof inspection myself?**
You can catch obvious signs from the ground, but a full inspection requires getting on the roof safely and checking for damage that isn't visible from below, like cracked shingle mat or compromised flashing seals.

**What if I don't see any damage but my neighbors are getting roofs replaced?**
It's worth getting checked anyway. Roof damage isn't always visible from the ground, and a professional inspection can confirm whether your roof escaped the storm or has damage you haven't spotted yet.
